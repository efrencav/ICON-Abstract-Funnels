Positioning Statement Builder

A structured workspace to draft your positioning statement component by component, assemble it into a final statement, and pressure-test it before putting it to work.

By Abstract Creative For Founders & Principals Pair with The Positioning Framework Time 60–90 min

How to use this template

Work through Parts 1–5 in sequence. Each part corresponds to one component of the Positioning Framework. Fill in your answers using the write lines, then carry them forward into the Assembly section to construct your full positioning statement.

When you're done, run the statement through the Pressure Test in Part 6. If it fails any test, use the Revision section to rework the specific component that broke.

Part 5 — Assemble Your Statement

Pull your summaries from Parts 1–4 into the formula below, then write your full statement in your own voice.

We help [audience from Part 1]
who are dealing with [problem from Part 2]
by [differentiator from Part 3] —
so they can [outcome / opposite of the problem].

Your positioning statement — write it in your voice, not the formula's

Part 6 — Pressure Test

Run your statement through each test before using it anywhere. Check Pass or Fail for each.

1

The one-read test

Read it to someone outside your industry. Can they tell you who it's for and what makes it different — without asking a follow-up question?

2

The team test

Ask two people on your team to describe the firm without prep. Does their answer reflect this statement? If not, the statement isn't landing internally yet.

3

The competitor test

Replace your firm's name with a direct competitor's. Does it still hold? If yes, you have a category description, not a positioning statement. Return to Part 3.

4

The self-recognition test

If your ideal client read this, would they immediately recognize themselves in it — and feel like it was written for them specifically?

5

The homepage test

Read your current website headline. Does it reflect this statement? If there's a gap, the site needs to be updated — not the positioning.

Revision notes

If a test failed, note which component needs reworking and what specifically needs to change. Return to that Part and revise before reassembling.
