Week 1 — Your website isn't the problem

Post Tuesday or Wednesday morning
Positioning Truth Tue / Wed 8–10am CT
Hook: "Your website isn't the problem."
Your website isn't the problem. Most founders think a redesign will fix conversions. It won't. Here's what I see over and over: The site looks great. The copy is polished. The traffic is decent. But the positioning is unclear. Visitors land and can't answer: → What exactly does this company do? → Who is it for? → Why them over anyone else? When those three questions don't have instant answers, people leave. Not because the design failed. Because the strategy did. A new website built on weak positioning just makes the confusion look more expensive. Fix the foundation first. Then build the site. —— I work with $500K–$5M firms in Houston and beyond to lock positioning before anything gets designed. If this sounds familiar, the assessment link is in my bio.

Week 3 — Brand breaks at $500K–$5M

Post Tuesday or Wednesday morning
Positioning Truth Tue / Wed 8–10am CT
Hook: "There's a stage almost every founder hits."
There's a stage almost every founder hits. Revenue is real. The team is growing. But something feels off. The brand that got you here is starting to work against you. Pitches feel harder than they should. The website embarrasses you in the wrong rooms. New team members have no idea how to talk about the company. This isn't a design problem. It's a scaling problem. At $0–$500K, hustle and founder charisma carry the brand. At $500K–$5M, that stops being enough. The buyers get more sophisticated. The sales cycles get longer. The decisions get made by committees, not individuals. And a brand that can't communicate value without the founder in the room becomes the ceiling. I've seen this pattern in Houston and across the country. The fix is always the same: lock the foundation before scaling anything else. —— Abstract Creative helps growth-stage firms close the gap between where their brand is and where their business needs it to be.

Week 4 — The ICON Method

Post Tuesday or Wednesday morning
ICON Method Education Tue / Wed 8–10am CT
Hook: "Every brand engagement I've run that delivered real results moved in the same order."
Every brand engagement I've run that delivered real results moved in the same order. I didn't plan it that way. I just noticed the pattern after enough projects. The ones that worked: 1. Identity first. Who you serve, what gap you close, why you win. Before anything gets designed. 2. Communication second. Visual system, messaging, brand standards. Built on the strategy — not ahead of it. 3. Optimization third. Website, landing pages, conversion paths. Designed around positioning — not just aesthetics. 4. Navigation last. CRM, funnels, campaigns, ongoing governance. Only valuable when the foundation holds. The projects that didn't work? They skipped steps or reversed the order. Built a website before positioning was clear. Ran ads before the message was locked. Order matters more than effort. —— This is the ICON Method — the framework behind every Abstract Creative engagement. Link in bio if you want the full breakdown.

Week 5 — More marketing won't fix positioning

Post Tuesday or Wednesday morning
Contrarian / Hot Take Tue / Wed 8–10am CT
Hook: "Unpopular opinion: Most founders don't have a marketing problem."
Unpopular opinion: Most founders don't have a marketing problem. They have a positioning problem they're trying to outspend. More ads. More content. More campaigns. All running on a message that was never clearly defined. Weak positioning doesn't get fixed with more volume. It just reaches more people who don't understand what you do. The founders who scale past $5M almost always did one thing differently: They stopped marketing harder and started being clearer. Clear on who they serve. Clear on what problem they solve. Clear on why they're the right answer. Marketing scales that message. It cannot create it. —— Houston branding strategist. I help growth-stage firms get clear before they scale.

Week 6 — Brand inconsistency costs money

Post Tuesday or Wednesday morning
Client Pattern Tue / Wed 8–10am CT
Hook: "Nobody talks about the cost of brand inconsistency."
Nobody talks about the cost of brand inconsistency. But it shows up everywhere: → A sales rep describes the company one way. The website says something different. The proposal uses a third version. → The prospect is confused. Confusion creates hesitation. Hesitation loses deals. This isn't a hypothetical. I've walked into companies where three people on the same team gave me three different answers to the question: "What does your company do?" No one noticed. Because everyone was too close to it. But buyers notice immediately. Brand inconsistency is a revenue problem. It just disguises itself as a marketing problem. —— If you're not sure how consistent your brand actually is, the free assessment in my bio takes 5 minutes.
